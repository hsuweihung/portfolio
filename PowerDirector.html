Summary

Subtitles are a core feature for video editors. This project focused on improving the overall efficiency and flexibility of the Subtitle Room through UX structure refinement and UI optimization. Building on existing functionality, the redesign reorganized workflows and interface structure to enhance efficiency, usability, and visual consistency in subtitle editing.

Outcome Example

# Landing Page

Strategy 1: Toolbar Redesign for clearer navigation structure

The toolbar was reserved for essential global actions, with secondary controls relocated to the side panel to clarify the primary navigation structure. And also combine Merge and Split into one function to reduce visual clutter.

Strategy 2: Make Add Subtitle more feasible

In the previous version, subtitles could only be added at the playhead position.In the new design, subtitles can be added either at the playhead or after a selected subtitle, giving users more flexibility when adding subtitles.

Strategy 3: Separate Subtitle List and Properties page for better organization

Dividing the subtitle management into two distinct pages: a Subtitle List page for overview and selection, and a Properties page for detailed editing and settings, enhancing organization and usability.

# Search, Find and Replace

Strategy: Separate Search and Replace to Clarify User Intent

In the previous design, after entering a search query, there was no clear visual cue indicating that users needed to click Find to initiate the search. Additionally, expanding the replace field caused unnecessary visual disruption in the interface. To improve clarity and usability, Search and Find & Replace were separated into two distinct functions, making the interaction more predictable and easier to understand.

Self-reflections

This project provided valuable lessons on balancing user experience, technical constraints, and iterative decision-making in a mature product environment. Through the Subtitle Room revamp, I gained deeper insights in the following areas:

1. Improving UX within long-term technical constraints: While many design opportunities could have further enhanced the user experience, the product’s accumulated technical debt over more than a decade limited the scope of fundamental changes. Rather than pursuing ideal but impractical solutions, I worked closely with R&D to evaluate feasibility and identify approaches that delivered the highest UX impact within realistic technical boundaries. This experience reinforced the importance of pragmatic design decisions that balance vision with implementation constraints.

2. Data-informed decisions do not guarantee perfect outcomes: Although usage data showed that AI Speech-to-Text adoption significantly exceeded manual subtitle creation, removing the control for adding subtitles at the playhead in the first release led to unexpected user complaints. This highlighted that while data is essential for identifying dominant behaviors, it cannot fully capture all usage contexts. The experience emphasized the need to validate assumptions through post-launch feedback and remain flexible in adjusting design decisions.

3. Design is an iterative process, not a one-time solution: There was no single “right” solution from the outset. Early explorations included tab-based layouts and interaction models that revealed settings only after entering edit modes. Through multiple iterations and cross-functional discussions, the final decision was to place related options in the left navigation. Although this introduced a new layout structure, it resulted in a more intuitive and discoverable experience for users, demonstrating that effective design often emerges through continuous refinement rather than initial certainty.
